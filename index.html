- Manuela Lasry Garrido aka Lola Gee

While big brands fight the most iconic and most charitable battles on the media, 30 cows feed off of wrapping papers-dressed lawn that Balinese neighbors dispose in the once green yard. On Instagram, the most selling rapper only wears Gucci while your little cousin has 10k plays on his self-directed rap video on YouTube. Alexander Wang is sued over his sweatshop working conditions, while I replicate Nike Air Max in rubber castings in my The Hague studio. Idols are born everyday and change with each wave of new hype. Everybody wants to get a slice of the rich-and- famous cake. Technological advancement has opened infinite possibilities, allowing for fast-paced approaches aimed at quick results. The end-result: a whole load of shitty attempts. The dilution of the original. Uninformation. Endless yards of trash. That’s my starting point. My work draws inspiration from the virtual public space (Internet and social media) and the physical public space. I view both spaces through the lense of mainstream culture and my work is fueled by the appropriation of its dynamics: DIY videos, bootlegs, plastic casting, iPhone snaps. My work is reaction to popular phenomena. It aims at creating new spaces which lay right at the intersection between the physical and the virtual. These spaces are created to start dialogue and/or protest about current topics. Its satirical. Its absurd. Its raw. At first glance my work is humorous, colorful and loud but it encloses a critical view. I am not attached to any medium: the medium depends strictly on the idea. I use installations and performance as my primary weapons of choice. Often a work becomes whole when composed by a collection of works that touch on the different aspects of the same experience. My working process is intuitive. Improvisation and spontaneity are qualities I embrace to maintain the human-ness quality near to the spectator.
